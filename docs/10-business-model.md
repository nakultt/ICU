## ROI Analysis — 100-Bed ICU Hospital

### Assumptions
```
Hospital:         100 ICU beds
Occupancy:        60% average (60 patients)
Avg ICU stay:     8 days
Avg ICU cost:     ₹50,000/day ($600/day) for the hospital
Annual admissions: ~2,700 patients (60 beds × 365 / 8 days avg stay)
```

### VisiCare Annual Cost
```
License:          ₹75,000/mo × 12 = ₹9,00,000 ($10,800)
Additional mins:  ~₹1,50,000/year (overage above included minutes)
Total:            ₹10,50,000/year ($12,600)
```

### Projected Savings from Reduced ICU Stay
Based on clinical evidence: virtual family visits reduce average ICU stay by **1.0–1.5 days** per patient.

```
Conservative (1.0 day reduction):
  2,700 patients × 1.0 day × ₹50,000/day = ₹13,50,00,000 saved
  ($1,620,000 USD)

Moderate (1.5 day reduction):
  2,700 patients × 1.5 days × ₹50,000/day = ₹20,25,00,000 saved
  ($2,430,000 USD)
```

### ROI Summary
| Metric | Conservative | Moderate |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| Annual VisiCare Cost | ₹10,50,000 | ₹10,50,000 |
| Annual ICU Savings | ₹13,50,00,000 | ₹20,25,00,000 |
| **Net Savings** | **₹13,39,50,000** | **₹20,14,50,000** |
| **ROI** | **128x** | **192x** |

### Additional Non-Quantified Benefits
- Reduced readmission rates (lower PTSD/depression post-discharge)
- Higher patient satisfaction scores (HCAHPS improvement)
- Reduced nursing burnout (fewer family confrontations)
- Competitive differentiator for hospital marketing
- Reduced litigation risk (better family communication)

## Revenue Projections (India Market)

### Total Addressable Market (TAM)
```
India ICU beds: ~300,000 (2024)
Hospitals with ICU: ~15,000
Target price: ₹75,000/mo average
TAM = 15,000 × ₹75,000 × 12 = ₹13,500 crore/year ($1.6B)
```

### Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM)
```
Private hospitals with 20+ ICU beds: ~5,000
SAM = 5,000 × ₹75,000 × 12 = ₹4,500 crore/year ($540M)
```

### Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM) — Year 1–3
```
Year 1: 20 hospitals  → ARR ₹1.8 crore ($216K)
Year 2: 100 hospitals → ARR ₹9.0 crore ($1.08M)
Year 3: 300 hospitals → ARR ₹27 crore ($3.24M)
```

## Go-to-Market Strategy

### Phase 1: Pilot & Prove (Month 1–6)
- Partner with 3–5 hospitals in Chennai/Bangalore for free pilots
- Collect clinical data on ICU stay reduction
- Publish case study / white paper

### Phase 2: Regional Launch (Month 6–12)
- Target top 50 multi-specialty hospitals in South India
- Hospital conferences: ISCCM, IMA events
- Direct sales team (2 reps) focused on ICU department heads

### Phase 3: National Scale (Year 2)
- Expand to North India (Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata)
- Partnership with hospital management companies (Apollo, Fortis, Max)
- Government hospital tenders (AIIMS, state medical colleges)

### Phase 4: International (Year 3+)
- Southeast Asia (similar demographics/bandwidth challenges)
- Middle East (large expat populations far from families)
- Enable GDPR compliance for EU expansion
